RIG‑7 — the harpoon tread‑bot

A legless redesign of the player, built to be loved. One big expressive eye is the whole face — it scans around when idle, narrows with determination when firing, and breaks into a happy squint when it pops a bubble. A fluttering coral scarf gives it a signature and trails in the wind. Underneath, the animation is honest physics: a planted tread that rolls, a hull that rocks on suspension, a cannon that recoils on a real spring, and a lagging antenna for follow-through. Every shape is drawn in code with one light source, and a single function — drawBot() — runs every state.

Porting into player.ts

Replace the body of Player.draw() with a single drawBot(ctx, this.x, GROUND_Y, pose) call. Build pose straight from the state you already track — no new systems:

  • facing ← your facing (only flips the head/eye + dust; the turret stays vertical).
  • vx ← your vx (signed). Drives the suspension lean and the dust kick.
  • dist ← accumulate dist += vx*dt (signed, not abs — that's what makes the wheels and tread reverse when moving left). This alone spins the wheels and scrolls the tread at exactly the right rate and direction.
  • fireT ← a tiny timer set to 0 in tryShoot(), advanced by dt each frame; pass -1 when not firing. The whole anticipation→launch→spring recovery is a pure function of it.
  • pal ← equippedPalette().colors. The P2 teal recolor (and any future skin) just works — the skin toggle above proves it.
  • cheerT (optional) ← set to 0 on a successful pop and advance by dt; pass -1 otherwise. Drives the happy-eye squint and a little hop — free emotional payoff every time the player scores.

drawBot() returns the muzzle point {x,y} at the barrel tip, so spawn your Projectile there and the spear lines up with no seam. The in-flight harpoon reuses your existing drawSpearhead helper from projectile.ts (included here, unmodified).
